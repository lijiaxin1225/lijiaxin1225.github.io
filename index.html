About Me

Hello! My name is Jiaxin Li, and you may call me Linda as well! I am a Master of Financial Engineering student at Cornell University. I finished my undergraduate studies majoring in Financial Mathematics and Statistics in June 2024 at University of California - Santa Barbara. I am passionate about quantitative research, data analytics, and financial engineering applications.

Projects

  • Multi-Period Liquidity-Constrained Portfolio Optimization – Designed a sector-aware optimization model minimizing liquidity-adjusted Value-at-Risk (LVaR) across 55 U.S. equities. Implemented asset- and regime-specific constraints based on trading volume. Automated data collection and rebalancing with yfinance and CVXPY, outperforming standard benchmarks in back-tests.
  • Quantitative Finance Project – Developed and back-tested trading strategies using CAPM, mean-variance frontier, and machine learning models (Random Forests, LASSO, SVM). Created market-neutral portfolios and leveraged alternative data to enhance out-of-sample performance.
  • Industry Drift Research – Used similarity matrices and clustering techniques to track Apple Inc.'s correlation with sector ETFs, revealing a shift from technology to consumer discretionary. Research forms the basis of an academic paper on portfolio strategy implications.
